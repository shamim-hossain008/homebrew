What Does Homebrew Do?


Homebrew installs the stuff you need that Apple (or your Linux system) didn’t.

Homebrew installs packages to their own directory and then symlinks their files into /opt/homebrew (on Apple Silicon).

Trivially create your own Homebrew packages.

It’s all Git and Ruby underneath, so hack away with the knowledge that you can easily revert your modifications and merge upstream updates.

Homebrew complements macOS (or your Linux system). Install your RubyGems with gem and their dependencies with brew.

“To install, drag this icon…” no more. Homebrew Cask
installs macOS apps, fonts and plugins and other non-open source software.
